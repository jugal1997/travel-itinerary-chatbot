5-Day Paris Itinerary - Classic Paris Experience

Trip Overview:
This 5-day itinerary covers Paris's iconic landmarks, world-class museums, charming neighborhoods, and culinary delights. Designed for first-time visitors who want to experience the best of Paris without feeling rushed.

Budget Estimate:
Mid-range budget: €150-250 per day per person including accommodation, meals, transportation, and attractions.

Day 1: Arrival and Eiffel Tower Area

Morning:
- Arrive in Paris, check into hotel
- Get Paris Visite travel pass or Navigo card for unlimited metro
- Quick breakfast at local café - croissant and coffee (€5-8)

Afternoon:
- Lunch in 7th arrondissement near Eiffel Tower (€15-25)
- Visit Eiffel Tower (book tickets online in advance to skip lines)
- Best time: 2-3 PM to avoid crowds
- Take elevator to 2nd floor (€17) or summit (€26)
- Spend 1.5-2 hours exploring and enjoying views

Late Afternoon/Evening:
- Walk through Champ de Mars gardens
- Cross Pont d'Iéna bridge for photo opportunities
- Explore Trocadéro Gardens for iconic Eiffel Tower views
- Dinner at bistro in 16th arrondissement (€25-40)
- Return to Eiffel Tower at night to see sparkling lights (every hour on the hour for 5 minutes)

Tips: Book Eiffel Tower tickets 2-3 months in advance online. Bring a light jacket for windy conditions at the top.

Day 2: Louvre and Tuileries

Morning:
- Breakfast at hotel or local boulangerie
- Arrive at Louvre when it opens (9 AM) to beat crowds
- Enter through less crowded Porte des Lions entrance
- Focus on highlights: Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, Winged Victory, Napoleon's apartments
- Allow 3-4 hours minimum (€17 entry)

Lunch:
- Café in Louvre courtyard or nearby Palais Royal (€15-25)
- Alternative: Picnic in Tuileries Gardens with items from nearby market

Afternoon:
- Stroll through Tuileries Gardens
- Visit Orangerie Museum for Monet's Water Lilies (€12.50, 1 hour)
- Walk through Place de la Concorde
- Explore Champs-Élysées (window shopping and people watching)

Evening:
- Climb Arc de Triomphe for sunset views (€13, 30 minutes)
- Dinner in nearby street (€30-50)
- Optional: Evening river cruise on Seine (€15-20, 1 hour)

Tips: Download Louvre map ahead. Louvre is closed Tuesdays. Buy Museum Pass if visiting multiple museums.

Day 3: Montmartre and Sacré-Cœur

Morning:
- Breakfast in hotel or local café
- Take metro to Anvers or Abbesses station
- Walk up to Sacré-Cœur Basilica (free entry)
- Climb dome for panoramic views (€7, optional)
- Spend 1-2 hours in basilica and surrounding area

Late Morning:
- Explore Montmartre neighborhood
- Visit Place du Tertre (artists' square)
- Find the "I Love You" wall
- Walk past Moulin Rouge (exterior only during day)
- Coffee break at café with view (€5-10)

Afternoon:
- Lunch at traditional French bistro in Montmartre (€20-35)
- Visit Musée de Montmartre (€12, small museum about neighborhood history)
- Walk through vineyards of Montmartre
- Explore quirky shops and art galleries

Evening:
- Descend to Pigalle neighborhood
- Dinner at local restaurant (€25-40)
- Optional: Cabaret show at Moulin Rouge (€90-200) or alternative venue
- Night stroll to see neighborhood nightlife

Tips: Wear comfortable shoes for hill walking. Beware of street scam artists in tourist areas. Visit Sacré-Cœur early morning or evening for fewer crowds.

Day 4: Latin Quarter, Notre-Dame, and Le Marais

Morning:
- Breakfast near hotel
- Visit Notre-Dame Cathedral exterior (currently closed for restoration, reopening 2024)
- Explore Île de la Cité and Sainte-Chapelle (€11.50, stunning stained glass)
- Walk along Seine riverbanks

Late Morning:
- Explore Latin Quarter
- Visit Panthéon (€11.50, 1 hour)
- Wander through narrow medieval streets
- Browse bookstores including famous Shakespeare and Company

Lunch:
- Traditional French lunch in Latin Quarter (€20-30)
- Try crêperie for authentic experience (€10-18)

Afternoon:
- Cross to Le Marais neighborhood
- Visit Place des Vosges (Paris's oldest square)
- Explore Jewish Quarter and its bakeries
- Shop in trendy boutiques and vintage stores
- Visit Musée Picasso if interested (€14, 1.5 hours)

Evening:
- Aperitif at rooftop bar with views
- Dinner in Le Marais (excellent restaurants for all budgets €15-50)
- Evening walk through illuminated medieval streets
- Stop for gelato or dessert at patisserie

Tips: Marais is great for shopping and LGBTQ-friendly nightlife. Many shops closed Sundays. Try falafel in Jewish Quarter (L'As du Fallafel).

Day 5: Versailles Day Trip and Final Evening in Paris

Morning:
- Early breakfast (7-8 AM)
- Take RER C train to Versailles (€7.10 round trip, 40 minutes)
- Arrive at Palace of Versailles when it opens (9 AM)
- Explore Palace interiors (2-3 hours)
- See Hall of Mirrors, Royal Apartments, Chapel

Late Morning/Early Afternoon:
- Lunch at café near palace or bring picnic for gardens (€15-30)
- Explore Gardens of Versailles (free except musical fountain days)
- Visit Marie Antoinette's Estate and Trianon Palaces
- Rent golf cart or bike if tired (optional €34-40)
- Allow 2-3 hours for gardens

Late Afternoon:
- Return to Paris (arrive 4-5 PM)
- Rest at hotel or freshen up

Evening - Final Night:
- Seine River sunset cruise (6:30-7:30 PM, €15-25)
- Farewell dinner at traditional French restaurant (€40-80)
- Recommendations: Duck confit, beef bourguignon, or coq au vin
- End with dessert: Crème brûlée or tarte tatin
- Final night walk - perhaps to Eiffel Tower one last time

Tips: Book Versailles tickets online in advance (€20). Palace closed Mondays. Musical fountain shows on weekends (extra €10). Wear very comfortable shoes. Versailles ticket includes palace, gardens, and Trianon.

Practical Information:

Getting Around:
Metro and buses cover entire city. Buy carnet (10 tickets) for €16.90 or multi-day pass. Metro runs 5:30 AM - 1:15 AM (2 AM weekends).

Where to Stay:
Best areas for tourists: Le Marais (central, trendy), Latin Quarter (historic, central), Montmartre (charming, nightlife), Saint-Germain-des-Prés (upscale, central).

Money Saving Tips:
- Museum Pass (€62 for 2 days, €78 for 4 days) saves money and time
- Picnic lunches with market ingredients
- Free museum entry first Sunday of each month
- Avoid restaurants directly at tourist sites
- Drink tap water (safe and free)

What to Pack:
Comfortable walking shoes (essential), light jacket, scarf (fashionable and practical), power adapter, reusable water bottle, small day bag for metro/attractions.

French Phrases:
Bonjour (Hello), Merci (Thank you), S'il vous plaît (Please), Pardon (Excuse me), L'addition s'il vous plaît (Check please), Combien ça coûte? (How much?), Où est...? (Where is...?)

Safety:
Paris is generally safe but watch for pickpockets especially on metro and near attractions. Keep valuables secure. Avoid unlicensed taxis. Be wary of petition scams and ring scams near monuments.
