Paris - The City of Light

Overview:
Paris is the capital and most populous city of France. Known for its art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture, Paris is one of the world's most visited destinations. The city receives approximately 30 million visitors annually.

Best Time to Visit:
The best time to visit Paris is from June to August and September to October. Spring (April to June) offers mild weather and blooming gardens. Fall (September to November) provides comfortable temperatures and fewer crowds. Summer is peak tourist season with warm weather but crowded attractions.

Top Attractions:
- Eiffel Tower: The iconic iron lattice tower stands 330 meters tall and offers breathtaking views of the city. Visit at sunset for magical views. Entry costs €25-35 for adults.
- Louvre Museum: The world's largest art museum houses over 35,000 works including the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo. Budget 3-4 hours minimum. Tickets cost €17.
- Notre-Dame Cathedral: Currently under restoration after the 2019 fire, but still worth viewing from outside. Expected to reopen in 2024.
- Arc de Triomphe: Honors those who fought for France. Climb 284 steps to the top for panoramic views. Entry €13.
- Sacré-Cœur Basilica: Located on Montmartre hill, this stunning white church offers free entry and spectacular city views.
- Champs-Élysées: Famous avenue for shopping and dining, stretching from Place de la Concorde to Arc de Triomphe.

Food and Dining:
Paris is a gastronomic paradise. Must-try foods include croissants, macarons, escargot, coq au vin, and crème brûlée. Budget €15-25 for casual meals, €50-100 for fine dining. Visit local bakeries (boulangeries) for authentic pastries. The best neighborhoods for food are Le Marais, Saint-Germain-des-Prés, and Montmartre.

Transportation:
The Paris Metro is efficient and affordable with a single ticket costing €1.90. Buy a carnet (10 tickets) for €16.90 to save money. The metro runs from 5:30 AM to 1:15 AM. Consider a Paris Visite pass for unlimited travel. Taxis are expensive; Uber is a more economical option. Walking is the best way to experience Parisian neighborhoods.

Accommodation:
Budget options: Hostels in Latin Quarter or Montmartre (€25-50/night)
Mid-range: Hotels in Marais or Saint-Germain (€100-200/night)
Luxury: Hotels near Champs-Élysées or Opera (€300+/night)

Budget Estimates:
- Budget traveler: €60-80/day (hostel, metro, street food, free attractions)
- Mid-range: €150-250/day (3-star hotel, restaurants, paid attractions)
- Luxury: €400+/day (4-5 star hotels, fine dining, taxis)

Local Tips:
- Learn basic French phrases; locals appreciate the effort
- Most museums offer free entry on the first Sunday of each month
- Avoid eating near major tourist attractions; walk 2-3 blocks for better prices
- Purchase museum passes online to skip queues
- Be cautious of pickpockets especially on metro and near tourist sites

Weather:
Spring (March-May): 10-20°C, mild and pleasant
Summer (June-August): 15-25°C, warm and sunny
Fall (September-November): 10-20°C, cool and crisp
Winter (December-February): 3-8°C, cold and occasionally rainy
