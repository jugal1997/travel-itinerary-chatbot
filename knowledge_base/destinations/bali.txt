Bali - Island of the Gods

Overview:
Bali is an Indonesian island known for its forested volcanic mountains, iconic rice paddies, beaches, and coral reefs. It's a spiritual and cultural haven offering yoga retreats, ancient temples, and vibrant arts scene alongside adventure activities and nightlife.

Best Time to Visit:
Dry season (April-October) is ideal, with peak season July-August. Shoulder seasons (April-June, September-October) offer good weather with fewer crowds and better prices. Rainy season (November-March) brings afternoon showers but also lush landscapes and lowest prices.

Top Regions and Attractions:
- Ubud: Cultural heart of Bali. Visit Sacred Monkey Forest, Tegallalang Rice Terraces, art galleries, and yoga studios. Perfect for culture and relaxation.
- Seminyak: Upscale beach town with trendy restaurants, beach clubs, boutique shopping, and sunset bars. Best for luxury and nightlife.
- Canggu: Surfer paradise with laid-back vibe, beach clubs, and digital nomad community. Mix of rice fields and beach.
- Uluwatu: Dramatic cliffs, world-class surf breaks, and stunning Uluwatu Temple. Watch the famous Kecak fire dance at sunset.
- Nusa Penida: Day trip island with Instagram-famous spots like Kelingking Beach and Angel's Billabong. Requires full day trip.
- Mount Batur: Active volcano offering sunrise trekking (4-5 hours, start 3-4 AM). Guide required, costs $30-50.

Activities:
- Surfing: Beginner-friendly breaks in Kuta, Canggu, advanced in Uluwatu
- Diving and Snorkeling: Amed, Nusa Penida, Tulamben wreck dive
- Yoga and Wellness: Ubud is yoga capital with numerous retreats and studios
- Temple Visits: Tanah Lot, Uluwatu, Besakih, Tirta Empul
- Rice Terrace Walks: Tegallalang and Jatiluwih UNESCO site
- Waterfalls: Tegenungan, Sekumpul, Gitgit

Food and Dining:
Must-try: Nasi Goreng (fried rice), Mie Goreng (fried noodles), Satay, Babi Guling (suckling pig), Bebek Betutu (duck), and fresh seafood. Local warungs offer meals for $2-5. Mid-range restaurants $10-20. Western food and beach clubs $20-50. Don't miss fresh tropical fruits and smoothie bowls.

Transportation:
Rent a scooter (70,000-100,000 IDR/day, ~$5-7) - international license required. Use Grab or Gojek (Indonesian Uber) for affordable rides. Private drivers for day trips cost $30-50. Traffic can be intense, especially in south Bali.

Accommodation:
Budget: Hostels and guesthouses ($10-25/night)
Mid-range: Hotels and villas with pools ($40-100/night)
Luxury: Private villas and 5-star resorts ($150-500+/night)

Budget Estimates:
- Budget: $25-40/day (guesthouse, local food, scooter, some activities)
- Mid-range: $60-100/day (nice hotel, mix of restaurants, tours)
- Luxury: $200+/day (private villa, fine dining, spa treatments)

Local Tips:
- Dress modestly when visiting temples; sarongs often provided
- Negotiate prices for tours, transport, and markets
- Avoid tap water; drink bottled or filtered water
- Be cautious of monkeys; don't show food or shiny objects
- Respect ceremony days when beaches may be closed
- Download offline maps; not all areas have good internet
- Get travel insurance that covers scooter accidents

Health and Safety:
- Get vaccines: Hepatitis A, Typhoid, consider Japanese Encephalitis
- Protect against mosquitoes (dengue fever risk)
- Be careful with street food hygiene
- Keep valuables secure; petty theft exists in tourist areas
- Respect ocean currents and rip tides

Weather:
Dry Season (April-October): 27-30°C, sunny with occasional showers
Rainy Season (November-March): 26-29°C, afternoon/evening rain, humid
Best months: May, June, September - good weather, fewer crowds
